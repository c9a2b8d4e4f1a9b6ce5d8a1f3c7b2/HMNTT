# Multiple Functionalities Will Break If USDC Turns On Fee-On-Transfer

## Summary
The report is about a bug in a deposit contract. When depositing funds, the contract uses a specific code to transfer the funds and update the balances. However, if the USDC currency has a certain feature turned on, the amount of funds transferred into the contract may be less than the amount recorded in the balances. This can cause problems when trying to withdraw funds, as there may not be enough funds in the contract. The recommendation is to calculate the difference between the amount of USDC before and after the transfer to ensure accurate accounting. The bug has been resolved.

## Details
**Severity** - Medium

**Status** - Resolved

**Description**

In the deposit contract , when depositing we perform the following →
```solidity
IERC20(usdc).safeTransferFrom(msg.sender, address(this), _amount);
        balances[msg.sender] += _amount;
        emit Deposited(msg.sender, _amount);
```

But if USDC (which is upgradeable) turns on its feature for fee-on-transfer , the amount which is transferred into the contract would be less than the amount in the balances[] mapping , this accounting can break the entire deposit contract , since there are lesser funds in the contract as compared to the balances[] stored in the state it might lead to revert while withdrawing.

**Recommendation**:

Calculate the amount of USDC prior to the transfer and calculate the amount after the transfer and only account for the difference between the two.
