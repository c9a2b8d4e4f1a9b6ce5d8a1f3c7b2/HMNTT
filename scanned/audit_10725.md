# Governance can remove policy check due to upgradability

## Summary
This bug report outlines a vulnerability in the AddressProvider.sol code, located on line 77. The vulnerability allows governance to upgrade values for configurations, which can then be used to circumvent policy checks. This could lead to malicious behavior or mistakes that result in funds being lost or stolen. 

The recommendation is to make the values immutable, or to require an explicit Safe operation to upgrade to a new version. Both Brahma and Spearbit have acknowledged this recommendation.

## Details
## Severity: Medium Risk

## Context
AddressProvider.sol#L77

## Description
Governance has the possibility to upgrade the values for several configurations. This allows circumventing the Policy checks, due to a bug, mistake, or malicious behaviour. Combined with a lack of security or checks on other places (e.g. multisig signers, too low a threshold), funds could be lost or stolen.

- `_getAuthorizedAddress(_TRANSACTION_VALIDATOR_HASH)`
- `_getAuthorizedAddress(_TRUSTED_VALIDATOR_HASH)`
- `_getAuthorizedAddress(_POLICY_VALIDATOR_HASH)`

## Recommendation
Determine a path to make them immutable, or have a way where an explicit Safe operation (signed by owner) is required to upgrade to a new version.

## Brahma
Acknowledged, within the trust scope of governance. Given that the governance and team is known and not anonymous, that increases the deterrence of such attacks.

## Spearbit
Acknowledged.
