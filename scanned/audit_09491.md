# Centralization risk due to keeper

## Summary
This bug report is about a potential issue in a contract called WaveFactory.sol. The problem is that the address keeper, which is responsible for authorizing and configuring all the deployed wave contracts, could be a single person or entity. This creates a risk of centralization, meaning that one person or entity has too much control over the contracts. The recommendation is to use a multi-sig wallet, which requires multiple people to agree before making changes, to increase decentralization and reduce the risk of malicious actions or lost private keys.

## Details
**Severity**: Medium

**Status**: Acknowledged

**Description**

In Contract WaveFactory.sol, the address keeper seems to authorize and configure all of the deployed wave contracts. In case this address is an EOA, this will lead to centralization risk for all the deployed wave contracts.

**Recommendation**:  

Use a multi-sig wallet for the contract and the roles, to increase decentralization and minimize the risk of malicious admin or loss of private keys.
