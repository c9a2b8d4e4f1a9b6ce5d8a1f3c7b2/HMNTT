# [H-02] Ignoring withdrawal limits during accounts merging

## Summary
This bug report discusses an issue with the `CollateralPool.merge` function, which combines two different collateral pools. The severity of this bug is considered high, and its impact is also rated as high. The likelihood of this bug occurring is medium. 

The issue is that when the `merge` function is called, it ignores the withdrawal limits of the `child` pool. This means that after the merge, users can withdraw funds from the `parent` pool without any restrictions, even though the `child` pool may have had withdrawal limits in place. 

The code snippet provided in the report shows that the `merge` function transfers funds from the `child` pool to the `parent` pool, but it does not transfer the withdrawal limits. This means that users can withdraw funds from the `parent` pool as if they were still part of the `child` pool, even though they are not. 

To fix this issue, the report recommends transferring all withdrawal limits from the `child` pool to the `parent` pool during the merge process. This will ensure that the `parent` pool has the same withdrawal limits as the `child` pool, and users will not be able to withdraw funds without restrictions.

## Details
**Severity**

**Impact:** High

**Likelihood:** Medium

**Description**

The `CollateralPool.merge` merges collateral pool `child` into the collateral pool `parent` ignoring withdrawal limits of the `child` pool.
