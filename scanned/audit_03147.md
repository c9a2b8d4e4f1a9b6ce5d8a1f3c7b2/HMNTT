# setFlowLimit does not change the indices and interest rates

## Summary
This bug report is about a problem in the MiniPoolAddressProvider contract, specifically in lines 349-355. When the flow limit of a reserve in a mini pool is changed, it affects the virtual available liquidity. This means that the interest rates and indices for that reserve in the mini pool also need to be updated. Additionally, setting the flow limit to a non-zero value or resetting it to 0 can trigger the calculation of the minimum liquidity rate, which is another reason why the interest rates and indices should be updated when setFlowLimit is called. The recommendation is to ensure that when setFlowLimit is called, the interest rates and indices are also updated. The bug has been fixed in commit 696a5fa4 by Cod3x and verified by Spearbit.
