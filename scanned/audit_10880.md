# Privileged Roles and Access

## Summary
The client's contract, `MaviaToken.sol`, has two extremely powerful roles - `_EDITOR_ROLE` and `DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE`. These roles have the ability to transfer tokens, blacklist addresses, block transfers, and whitelist users, giving them control over the users and assets in circulation. The roles are assigned to the deployer upon deployment, along with the minting of four million Mavia tokens. The audit team recommends that the client clearly documents the roles and their responsibilities, reduces their power and centralization, and distributes them over multiple addresses or multi-sig in a transparent manner.

## Details

The audit team still recommends that the client updates documentation to specifically state the actions of all privileged roles.

**File(s) affected:**`MaviaToken.sol`

**Description:** In this contract, the `_EDITOR_ROLE` and `DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE` are extremely privileged. Their implicit and explicit capabilities include, but are not limited to:

1.   Transfer tokens between any two accounts. This can be achieved by setting a gateway address.
2.   Blacklist any address and prevent them from transferring tokens.
3.   Block further transfers for all users by increasing `tfStartTime` or setting `tfMaxAmount` to zero.
4.   Allow users to circumvent transfer restrictions enforced with `tfStartTime` and `tfMaxAmount`. This can be achieved by whitelisting that user.

These listed capabilities give both roles, `_EDITOR_ROLE` and `DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE`, the power to control the users and assets in circulation. Upon deployment, both roles are assigned to the deployer, as well as minting four million Mavia tokens as well.

**Recommendation:** Consider which abilities are needed by those roles to fulfill the application's use case. Clearly document each role, including their responsibilities and capabilities. Reduce the power and centralization of those roles. Further granularize roles and distribute them over several addresses or multi-sig in a transparent manner.
