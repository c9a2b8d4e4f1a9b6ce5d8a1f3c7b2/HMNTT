# Privileged Roles and Ownership

## Summary
A client has acknowledged a bug in the `ReachMainDistribution.sol`, `ReachAffiliateDistribution.sol`, and `ReachFactory.sol` files. These files have privileged permissions that are not clearly documented for users. The owner of these contracts has the ability to withdraw all contract balances, change prices, create new distributions, transfer ownership, and swap ETH for REACH tokens. The rewards withdrawal process is also controlled by the team, creating a risk of centralization. It is recommended that the impact of these privileged actions is made clear to users through publicly available documentation.

## Details
**Update**
Marked as "Acknowledged" by the client. The client provided the following explanation:

> Documentation on the website will be updated accordingly. These methods are necessary to keep the contracts working and customizable depending on how the protocol evolves

**File(s) affected:**`ReachMainDistribution.sol`, `ReachAffiliateDistribution.sol`, `ReachFactory.sol`

**Description:** All privileged permissions should be clearly documented for users. The privileged permissions of the audited contracts are documented below:

*   `ReachMainDistribution`:
    *   Owner can:
        *   Withdraw all the balance of the contract (ETH and REACH)
        *   Change credit price
        *   Create new distributions
        *   Transfer the ownership to another address
        *   Swap ETH balance for REACH tokens

*   `ReachAffiliateDistribution`:
    *   Owner can:
        *   Create new distributions
        *   Transfer the ownership to another address
        *   Swap ETH balance for REACH tokens

*   `ReachFactory`:
    *   Owner can:
        *   Deploy new affiliate distribution contracts
        *   Change `mainDistribution` address
        *   Change `commission` value
        *   Transfer the ownership to another address

Furthermore, the rewards withdrawal logic depends solely on Merkle roots provided by the off-chain component controlled by the team. This design allows the owner to provide rewards to targeted receivers but, at the same time, introduces centralization risks.

**Recommendation:** This centralization of power needs to be made clear to the users, especially depending on the level of privilege the contract allows to the owner. Clarify the impact of these privileged actions on the end-users via publicly facing documentation.
