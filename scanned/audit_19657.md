# [M-03] Validator can fail to receive commission reward in redeemAllRewards

## Summary
A bug has been reported in the DelegatedStaking.sol contract which could prevent validators from receiving their commission rewards. When the validator calls the `redeemAllRewards` function, there is a check which could cause the transaction to be reverted even if the validator had commission rewards to receive. This can happen if the commission rate is set to 1e18 - 1. This bug may be rarely encountered, and the validator could still redeem the reward through `redeemRewards`. As a recommendation, it is suggested to call `redeemRewards` in `redeemAllRewards` as a more succinct way to handle this issue.

## Vulnerability details

## Impact
Validator can fail to receive commission reward by calling `redeemAllRewards`.
There's a check in `redeemAllRewards`
```solidity
        uint128 rewards = sharesToTokens(s.shares, v.exchangeRate) - s.staked;
        require(rewards > 0, "Nothing to redeem");
```
The validator's tx might be reverted here even if he got some commission reward to receive.

## Proof of Concept
[DelegatedStaking.sol#L275-L276](https://github.com/code-423n4/2021-10-covalent/blob/main/contracts/DelegatedStaking.sol#L275-L276)
We can trigger the bug by setting commisionRate to 1e18 - 1

## Recommended Mitigation Steps

Though this may rarely happen and the validator can redeem the reward through `redeemRewards`, this may cause some issues when the validator is handled by a contract.

I consider calling `redeemRewards` in `redeemAllReawards` as a more succinct way to do this.
